Fluditeg

A compact thermoelectric generator with no moving parts.

What it does

Basing on the Peltier effect, Fluditeg transforms the heat produced by an endothermic energy, otherwise burnt into the atmosphere, in electrical energy.

How it works

Supplied with two fluids at different temperature (ranging from 60 to 200 °C), produces electric energy in relation to both, the amount of heat energy applied and the temperature delta between the fluids.

Why it is good

Amongst others it provides for: reduced fuel consumptions up to 3%, reduced emissions and no additional costs compared to an existing generator eventually replaced.

Durability

It is just made up of aluminum plates, wafer-assembled, accommodating two separate hydraulic circuits.

Efficiency

Recover 3.5% circa of the heat enery applied. For example, if 8600Kcal (10KWh) are applied, 350Wh are made available as electrical energy; such a device would be sized 12dm3 and outputting 300W/350W circa by using two fluids at a delta temperature of 75°C; it would weight 35Kg circa.

Usability

It could for example easily help recover what a typical liner bus burns off into the atmosphere, which is around 50% of the heat produced by its engine.
